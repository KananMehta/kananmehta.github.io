Novel Carbon-Fiber Robot Design

Throughout my junior year I worked as the main mechanical designer on a robotics project at Caltech’s Center for Autonomous Systems Technology. The postdoc leading the project had already designed some of the core components on his own but I added much of the detail to the joints and chassis, integrated the off-the-shelf components, and designed much of the electronics mounting and other components in the ‘head’ of the robot. I also designed a housing/coupler for a BLDC motor, encoder, and planetary gearbox that was intended to be a potential replacement for the harmonic drive motors if the manufacturer could not fill the order in time, and a wheeled testbed for safely testing the robot. Unfortunately I left to work at Lockheed Martin for the summer before the electronics and software were fully built.

Tensile Testing

The first set of solids simulations in this course was a simple demonstration of plastic and elastic deformations of an aluminum ‘dogbone’ plate. In the first plot we can clearly see the internal stress increasing until the material fails and begins to deform plastically and stress ceases increasing. In the second plot, a plate is again loaded linearly but stopped just short of failure. Although the material is still irreversibly deformed, it relaxes linearly, or elastically, to a significantly lower displacement than it was loaded to. For one extra variable, the simulations were performed on geometries both with and without a hole punched through the center. Unsurprisingly, the perforated plates failed at significantly lower loads due to the extreme stress that develops around the hole.

In the second term, this same test was performed on actual aluminum plates with a tensile testing apparatus and a high-speed camera for DIC (digital image correlation). Aside from generating the pretty strain plots below, I was also able to use Hooke’s law for stress-strain relation to derive an experimental estimate for the stress intensity factor, K. The K value of 2.336 was only off by under 1.4% from the theoretical value.

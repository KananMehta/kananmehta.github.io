Amphibious Tank Robot Design

For my senior capstone design project, I was put into a team of 5 to design and fabricate three robots for competition against our peers. The competition area was a pond divided into three sections separated by ramps, meaning we needed amphibious vehicles. The competition objectives involved picking up various colored balls from the arena and moving transporting them to specific zones to score points.

Our strategy was to design two large, expensive fully amphibious tank-boats that would be capable of storing as many balls as we could find. The third robot was made using the leftover budget and was a simple, lightweight fanboat with tiny wheels on the bottom for traversing ramps. Our large vehicles consisted of a Teflon-like HDPE thermoformed hull. It was fitted to CNC’ed aluminum plates with bolts and silicone gaskets. A shovel-like mechanism on the front and a moveable wall on the back were mounted to shafts and connected to linear actuators to allow them to collect and eject balls. The land locomotion system consisted of tank-like treads powered by a brushless motor and gear drive. The water propulsion used custom 3-D printed waterjets and impellers with brushless motors. The cheaper third robot was constructed almost entirely of lasercut ABS. The control systems all used Arduinos with hobbyist ESCs.

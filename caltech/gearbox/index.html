Tensioned Belt-Drive Gearbox

For my first serious mechanical design project my group of 3 students was tasked with developing a gearbox to transmit torque from a small electric motor to a bicycle wheel. We were provided dimensions for the spacing and height of the couplings, the mounting geometry on the test platform, a recommended gear ratio, and a budget, but were otherwise left to our own devices. While most groups went with a simple spur gear design we decided to go with a dual-inline pulley design with an added tensioner/idler pulley. The bearings, belts, pulleys, springs, and fasteners were purchased but the rest of the components were machined by hand (with the exception of the decorative Pokemon plate, which was laser-etched).

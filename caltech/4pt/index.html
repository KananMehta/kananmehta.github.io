4-Point Bending

For the 4-point load test, we only performed the real-life experiment. Similar to the cantilever experiment, we used an aluminum bar outfitted with a strain gage. Essentially the same analysis was performed as for the cantilever experiment, with the principal and shear strains being calculated from the strain gage data. Then with simple beam analysis the bending force and moment could be calculated, which were used to compute the bending stress at the top surface of the beam. This in turn could be used to compute Poisson’s ratio and the elastic modulus, resulting in an even more accurate estimate of 0.3246 for Poisson’s and an identical elastic modulus to the cantilever experiment.

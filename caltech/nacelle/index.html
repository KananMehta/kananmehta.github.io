Engine Nacelle Analysis

The second simulation for this course consisted of testing various mesh settings and CFD techniques for a straight pipe and an engine nacelle, allowing us to get more practice with ANSYS Fluent and learning to model axisymmetric flows.

The results were not as interesting as for the airfoil simulations. For the straight pipe I found that the steady-state inviscid model (Spalart-Allmaras) could not converge, while the transient inviscid and steady-state viscous models both converged and showed similar flow features as well as satisfying the continuity equation. Since this geometry was quite simple mesh type did not matter much.

For the engine nacelle, mesh density greatly affected the results. Once the mesh was properly refined, the Spalart-Allmaras model was found to be the best solution as it returned a much higher bypass ratio (due to the multiple boundary layers in close proximity, slowing flow through the center of the engine)

We again returned to the water tunnel to examine a 3-D printed engine nacelle similar to the one we modeled. Since this experiment involved running the water pump at different frequencies, we again collected data upstream of the test sample to experimentally determine the freestream velocity for each pump setting.
